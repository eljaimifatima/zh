My latest research paper was published in the 28th edition of the IEEE ISCC conference

I am excited to announce that my latest research paper on artificial intelligence has been published in the 28th edition of the IEEE ISCC conference, which is a Rank A prestigious conference . The paper is titled "An Integrated Framework for Bird Recognition Using Dynamic Machine Learning-Based Classification": Bird recognition in computer vision poses two main challenges: high intra-class variance and low inter-class variance. we propose a robust integrated framework for bird recognition using a dynamic machine learning-based technique. Our system is designed to identify over 11,000 species of birds based on multiple components. As part of this work, we propose two public datasets. The first one (E-Moulouya BDD) contains over 13k images of birds for detection tasks. While the second one (LaSBiRD) contains about 5M labelled images of 11k species. This yielded promising results, with a mAP of 0.715 for detection and an accuracy rate of 96% for classification.
